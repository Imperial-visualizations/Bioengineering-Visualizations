Ampere's Law

The magnetic field generated by an infinitely long line of current I is: $$ B= \frac{\mu I}{2\pi r} $$ Where μ = μ0 μr is the permitivity of the medium, r is the distance from the line to the point at which the magnetic field is evaluated.

By integrating around a closed loop, a useful law can be derived:

Ampere's circuital law:
$$ \oint_C \mathbf{B} . \mathbf{dl} = \mu I $$ $$\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu \mathbf{J}$$ Where J is the current density.
