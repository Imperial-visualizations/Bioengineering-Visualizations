Moments of Inertia - Theory

1. Introduction

The moment of Inertia(I), is a quantity used to represent a body's tendency to resist angular acceleration. It is sometimes referred to as the 'rotational equivalent' of mass for linear motion.
It is the sum of the mass of each article in the body with the square of the distance from the axis of rotation. For a point mass, it can be represented by the following equation:

$$I=m.d^2 $$

While mass is also an important component in resisting rotational motion, just as in linear motion,
it also shows a large dependence on the displacement of each point mass from the axis of rotation.
