Block Sliding on a Ramp - Worked Solution

mg= mass multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (Weight)
N= normal contact force
F= Frictional force

  1. Take x axis along slope and y axis perpendicular to slope
  2. Resolve the weight in the x and y axis to become \(mgcos(\theta)\) perpendicular to the slope and \(mgsin(\theta)\) along the slope.
  3. Resolve forces in the y axis: \(N - mgcos(\theta) = 0\) (as the mass is not accelerating in the y axis), therefore \(N = mgcos(\theta)\).
  4. Resolve forces in the x axis: \(mgsin(\theta) - F = 0\) (as the block is in equilibrium and is about to slip so no acceleration). Therefore \(F = mgsin(\theta)\)
  5. Apply Coulombs Law (\(F=(\mu)N\)) so that \((\mu)N = mgsin(\theta)\).
  6. Substitute in \(N\) into the equation in 5. so that \((\mu)mgcos(\theta) = mgsin(\theta)\).
  7. Cancel out the \(mg\) terms on both sides so \((\mu)cos(\theta) = sin(\theta)\).
  8. Divide through by \(cos(\theta)\) so \((\mu)= tan(\theta)\).
  9. The question is asking for the maximum angle and \(\mu\) is given so rearranging gives: \((\theta) = tan^-1(\mu)\)

As the \(\mu\) given for steel is 0.75, substituting that value into the equation gives a maximum angle \(\theta\) = 36.9\(\circ\).
